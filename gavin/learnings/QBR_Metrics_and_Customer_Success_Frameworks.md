## Executive Summary

Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs) are strategic touchpoints that demonstrate value, uncover expansion opportunities, and strengthen customer relationships. For Decklar's IoT supply chain visibility customers, effective QBRs must translate sensor data into business impact and ROI.

## Part 1: QBR Structure for Decklar Customers

### Standard QBR Agenda Template

| Time | Section | Owner | Purpose |
|------|---------|-------|---------|
| 0-5 min | Executive Summary | AM (Jeff) | Set context, health status, relationship pulse |
| 5-15 min | Deployment & Usage Review | AM + Customer Ops | Device activation rates, shipment coverage, data completeness |
| 15-30 min | Metrics & Value Proof | AM | Quantified outcomes: claims prevented, incidents caught, efficiency gains |

## Part 2: Core QBR Metrics Framework

### A. Deployment Health Metrics

**Activation & Coverage:**
- Device activation rate (% of purchased devices activated)
- Shipment coverage (% of total shipments tracked)
- Data completeness (% of shipments with full sensor data)
- Lane coverage (geographic spread of monitored routes)

**Technical Performance:**
- Device uptime / connectivity rate
- Average time to first ping (activation speed)
- Data latency (real-time vs. delayed reporting)
- Sensor accuracy (GPS, temperature, humidity readings)

**User Adoption:**
- Active user count vs. licensed users
- Login frequency (daily/weekly active users)
- Feature utilization (alerts, reports, dashboards accessed)
- Training completion rates

### B. Value Proof Metrics

**Claims & Risk Mitigation:**
- Claims reduction (YoY comparison)
- Incidents detected proactively (theft, damage, delays)
- Average time to incident resolution
- Cost per claim (trending down?)
- Insurance premium impacts (if tracked)

**Operational Efficiency:**
- Shipment visibility rate (% real-time tracked)
- Average detention/demurrage reduction
- Route optimization impact (fuel savings, time savings)
- Manual check-in reduction (labor hours saved)
- Customer service inquiry reduction (self-service adoption)

**Compliance & Quality:**
- Cold chain compliance rate (% within temp thresholds)
- Regulatory documentation completeness
- Quality hold incidents detected
- Product integrity scores

### C. Financial Impact Metrics

**Direct Savings:**
- Claims cost avoided ($)
- Expedited freight cost avoided ($)
- Spoilage/shrinkage reduction ($)
- Insurance premium savings ($)

**Efficiency Gains:**
- Labor hours saved (manual tracking eliminated)
- Expedited freight spend optimization ($)
- Inventory carrying cost improvements
- Working capital improvements (faster dispute resolution)

**Revenue Protection:**
- Customer retention risk mitigated
- SLA compliance improvements
- Brand reputation incidents avoided

## Part 4: Customer Success Playbook

### Health Scoring Framework

**Green (Healthy):**
- Device activation >90%
- Active usage >70% of licensed users
- Value metrics trending positive
- No critical open items >30 days
- Customer responsiveness: regular engagement

**Amber (At-Risk):**
- Device activation 50-90%
- Usage declining or low adoption
- Mixed value metrics (some positive, some flat)
- Open items >30 days
- Engagement gaps or delayed responses

**Red (Critical):**
- Device activation <50%
- Minimal usage or user churn
- Value not demonstrated
- Critical issues unresolved >60 days
- Communication breakdown or escalation risk

### Proactive Intervention Triggers

| Trigger | Action | Timeline |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Device activation <80% at T+30 | Adoption call + training | Immediate |
| No shipments tracked in 14 days | Health check call | Within 48 hours |
| Temperature excursions >5% | Alert review + threshold tuning | Same day |
| User logins declining >50% | Engagement survey + training | Within 1 week |
| Support tickets >3 in 30 days | Account review + SOP check | Within 1 week |
| Go-live >90 days without value proof | Executive business review | Immediate |

### Expansion Opportunity Signals

**Natural Expansion Triggers:**
- New facility/lane mentioned in calls
- Seasonal volume spikes
- M&A activity (new subsidiaries)
- New product lines requiring tracking
- Compliance requirement changes
- Competitor churn in their network

**Expansion Conversation Starters:**
- "I noticed you're adding a DC in Texas—shall we discuss tracking for those lanes?"
- "Your Q2 volume is up 40%—are you tracking all those shipments?"
- "You're expanding into pharmaceuticals—our Bee Labels have pharma mode enabled."

## Part 5: QBR Document Structure

### 1. Executive Summary (1 page)
- Health status with RAG indicator
- Relationship pulse (strengths, concerns)
- Key wins since last QBR
- Recommended focus for next quarter

### 2. Deployment Status (1-2 pages)
- Device inventory and activation rates
- Lane/geographic coverage map
- User adoption metrics
- Technical performance summary

### 3. Value Realization (2-3 pages)
- Claims/incident metrics with trends
- Operational efficiency gains
- Financial impact (cost avoided + efficiency gains)
- Case studies from the quarter

### 4. Open Items & Risk Register (1 page)
- Open items with owners and due dates
- Risks with mitigation status
- Blockers requiring executive attention

### 5. Expansion Roadmap (1 page)
- Identified opportunities
- Business case for each
- Timeline and next steps

### 6. Next Quarter Goals (1 page)
- Agreed priorities
- Success metrics
- Meeting cadence

## Part 6: Talking Points by Customer Stage

### Onboarding (0-90 days)
**Focus:** Activation, training, first wins
- "Let's get 100% of your devices activated by [date]"
- "We're targeting your first tracked shipment this week"
- "Training completion goal: [X] users by [date]"

### Growth (3-12 months)
**Focus:** Value proof, optimization, expansion
- "You've prevented $X in claims this quarter"
- "Your visibility coverage is at X%—let's get to 100%"
- "We see expansion opportunities in [area]"

### Mature (12+ months)
**Focus:** Strategic partnership, innovation, advocacy
- "You're in the top X% of customers for [metric]"
- "Let's explore advanced features: [list]"
- "Would you be open to a case study/reference call?"

## Part 7: Red Flags and Escalation Criteria

### Immediate Escalation Triggers
- Customer mentions "evaluating alternatives"
- Key stakeholder departure
- Regulatory compliance issue
- Data security concern
- Contract renewal discussion initiated by customer

### Soft Signals (Investigate)
- Repeated meeting reschedules
- New procurement/IT contacts involved
- Questions about pricing/contracts
- Competitor mentions
- Usage declining without explanation

## Key Takeaways

1. **QBRs are value-demonstration moments** — Lead with outcomes, not features
2. **Metrics tell the story** — Quantify everything: $ saved, incidents caught, efficiency gained
3. **Every QBR is a sales opportunity** — Expansion happens when value is proven
4. **Health scores predict behavior** — Monitor activation, usage, and engagement closely
5. **Proactive > Reactive** — Intervene at amber, not red
