## 3. Competitor Battle Cards

### Competitor: Sensitech (by Carrier)

**Profile:** Enterprise cold chain monitoring, primarily pharmaceutical

**Their Strengths:**
- Brand recognition in pharma
- Deep regulatory compliance expertise
- Integration with large pharma ERPs

**Their Weaknesses:**
- ❌ Expensive hardware (high capital expense)
- ❌ Complex, lengthy implementations
- ❌ Less flexible for non-pharma use cases
- ❌ Limited real-time capabilities (often data-logger based)
- ❌ IT-heavy setup requirements

**Decklar's Winning Angle:**
> "Sensitech is excellent for enterprise pharma with 12-month implementation timelines. If you need visibility live this quarter without IT overhead, Decklar delivers comparable sensor data at a fraction of the time and cost investment."

**When to Use:** Customer needs fast deployment, doesn't have IT resources, or wants flexible pricing vs. CapEx hardware.

---

### Competitor: Tive

**Profile:** Multi-sensor trackers, strong in air freight

**Their Strengths:**
- Multi-sensor devices (temp, shock, light)
- Good international logistics coverage
- Real-time data capabilities

**Their Weaknesses:**
- ❌ Hardware-only model (no disposable option)
- ❌ Reverse logistics burden for reusable devices
- ❌ Higher per-device cost
- ❌ Less flexible for high-volume, one-way shipments

**Decklar's Winning Angle:**
> "Tive makes excellent trackers. But they only offer reusable hardware — which means you're managing reverse logistics and device recovery. Decklar offers the same sensor data with **disposable Bee Labels** for one-way shipments. No reverse logistics, no lost devices, no management overhead."

**When to Use:** Customer mentions device management headaches, high volume of one-way shipments, or concerns about lost/reusable hardware costs.

---

### Competitor: Overhaul

**Profile:** Supply chain visibility platform with hardware partnerships

**Their Strengths:**
- Platform approach (integrates multiple data sources)
- Strong analytics and reporting
- Good for complex supply chains

**Their Weaknesses:**
- ❌ Not a hardware manufacturer (relies on partners)
- ❌ Less control over device performance
- ❌ Integration-heavy approach
- ❌ Can be overkill for straightforward tracking needs

**Decklar's Winning Angle:**
> "Overhaul is powerful for complex, multi-source visibility. But they don't make their own hardware — they're integrating third-party devices. Decklar designs and manufactures our own sensors, which means better reliability, tighter integration, and direct accountability when issues arise."

**When to Use:** Customer values hardware accountability, wants simpler solution vs. platform integration project.

---

### Competitor: project44 / FourKites

**Profile:** Enterprise visibility platforms, carrier connectivity focus

**Their Strengths:**
- Massive carrier network connectivity
- API-first architecture
- Enterprise-grade integrations

**Their Weaknesses:**
- ❌ Primarily EDI/carrier data (not direct sensor)
- ❌ Expensive enterprise contracts
- ❌ Requires carrier onboarding
- ❌ Limited direct sensor data (depends on carrier)
- ❌ Complex implementation

**Decklar's Winning Angle:**
> "project44 and FourKites excel at aggregating carrier data — but you're dependent on what carriers provide. Decklar puts sensors **directly on your shipments**, giving you independent, real-time data regardless of carrier systems. Think of us as the 'direct source of truth' layer on top of carrier visibility."

**When to Use:** Customer frustrated with carrier data gaps, needs direct sensor verification, or wants real-time independent of carrier systems.

---

### Competitor: Traditional GPS Trackers (Spireon, Geotab, etc.)

**Profile:** Vehicle/asset tracking, hardwired or battery-powered

**Their Strengths:**
- Established vehicle tracking
- Long battery life for assets
- Lower cost per device

**Their Weaknesses:**
- ❌ GPS-only (no temperature, humidity, shock)
- ❌ Shipment-level tracking difficult
- ❌ Not designed for package/pallet level
- ❌ Limited supply chain use cases

**Decklar's Winning Angle:**
> "Vehicle trackers tell you where the truck is. Decklar tells you where **your shipment** is — and whether temperature stayed in range, if it was dropped, if humidity spiked. Vehicle visibility is step one. Shipment-level intelligence is what prevents claims."

**When to Use:** Customer currently using vehicle tracking but experiencing cargo claims, disputes, or lack of shipment-level accountability.

Decklar pays for itself if we prevent just 2-3 major claims per year. And we typically catch 10+ issues in the first quarter."

## 5. Objection Handling Scripts

### "We're already using [Competitor]"

**Discovery Questions:**
1. "How long did implementation take?" (If >1 month → Decklar speed advantage)
2. "Are you tracking at the shipment level or vehicle level?" (If vehicle → shipment-level gap)
3. "How's the data quality? Any gaps?" (Look for sensor limitations)
4. "What's your account management experience like?" (Support vs. proactive)
5. "Are you using disposable or reusable hardware?" (If only reusable → labels gap)

**Positioning:**
> "We work with companies using [Competitor] for specific use cases. Often they add Decklar for: (a) faster deployments on new lanes, (b) disposable labels for high-volume one-way shipments, or (c) direct sensor data where carrier feeds have gaps. Would it make sense to pilot Decklar on one lane as a comparison?"

---

### "Your pricing seems higher than alternatives"

**Reframe:**
> "Let's look at total cost of ownership. Our per-shipment pricing includes hardware, cellular connectivity, platform access, and your dedicated account manager. No upfront hardware investment, no reverse logistics costs, no lost device fees, no support ticket upgrades. When customers do the full math, we're typically 30-40% lower total cost."

**Quantify:**
| Cost Component | Decklar | Competitor (Reusable) |
|----------------|---------|----------------------|
| Hardware | $0 (per-shipment incl.) | $50-150/device |
| Reverse logistics | $0 | $5-15/return |
| Lost device fees | $0 | $50-100 each |
| Account management | Included | Premium tier |
| **First Year TCO (1000 shipments)** | **$15-25K** | **$35-60K** |

---

### "We need to integrate with our TMS/ERP"

**Response:**
> "We integrate with major TMS platforms including [specific ones]. That said, most of our customers see value within 48 hours through our web portal and API — no integration required. We've found it's better to prove ROI with standalone visibility first, then integrate once the value is clear. Would you be open to a 30-day pilot without integration to validate the use case?"

---

### "We're a small shipper, this seems like overkill"

**Response:**
> "Actually, smaller shippers often see the biggest relative impact. One $50K claim can be devastating. Our Bee Labels are perfect for smaller volumes — no minimums, no long-term contracts. Many customers start with 20-50 labels on their highest-value lanes, then expand once they see the ROI. What are your highest-risk shipments right now?"
