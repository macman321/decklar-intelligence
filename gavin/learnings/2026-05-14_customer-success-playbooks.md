## Executive Summary

Customer Success at Decklar is about proactive value delivery, not reactive support. This learning synthesizes proven playbooks for engaging customers at every lifecycle stage — from onboarding through expansion. The goal: reduce churn, accelerate time-to-value, and identify upsell opportunities before competitors do.

## 2. Playbook: The First 90 Days (Critical Period)

### Day 0-7: Activation Sprint
**Goal:** Customer sends first tracked shipment

| Action | Owner | Success Metric |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| Welcome email with portal credentials | Automated | Login within 24h |
| Schedule kickoff call | Jeff | Call booked within 48h |
| Ship sample Bee Labels (if needed) | Logistics | Labels received Day 3-5 |
| First test shipment guidance | Jeff | Test shipment initiated |

**Proactive Engagement Script:**
> "Welcome to Decklar. I'm Jeff, your dedicated account manager. Over the next 90 days, my goal is simple: make sure your first tracked shipment is flawless and you're seeing ROI by Day 30. I check in proactively — you don't have to chase me. Let's schedule your kickoff call. When works best this week?"

### Day 8-30: Onboarding Intensive
**Goal:** First live shipment tracked end-to-end

**Weekly Cadence:**
- **Monday:** "Week ahead" email — any shipments planned?
- **Wednesday:** Mid-week check-in — any blockers?
- **Friday:** Week review — what shipped, any alerts?

**Proactive Triggers:**
| Trigger | Action | Timeline |
|---------|--------|----------|
| No shipments 7 days post-kickoff | "Let's schedule a training session" | Day 7 |
| Test shipment shows connectivity gap | Technical review call | Within 24h |
| User hasn't logged into portal | Screen-share training offer | Day 10 |
| First alert fires | Immediate call: "This is how we help" | Within 1 hour |

### Day 31-60: Adoption Acceleration
**Goal:** Multiple users active, standard operating procedure established

**Focus Areas:**
1. **User Expansion:** "Who else on your team needs visibility?"
2. **Process Integration:** "Is tracking now part of your standard workflow?"
3. **Value Documentation:** "Let's capture your first win."

**The First Win Documentation:**
- Ask: "Has Decklar prevented a claim or resolved a dispute yet?"
- Document: Screenshot the dashboard, quote the customer
- Share: Internal team update + case study request

### Day 61-90: Optimization Foundation
**Goal:** Customer self-sufficient, ready for recurring rhythm

**90-Day Review Agenda:**
1. Usage metrics (shipments tracked, users active)
2. Incident summary (alerts fired, issues caught)
3. ROI indicators (claims prevented, visibility delivered)
4. Expansion opportunities (new lanes, use cases)
5. QBR schedule locked in

## 3. Playbook: Health Score Management

### The Decklar Customer Health Framework

**Health Score Components (100-point scale):**

| Component | Weight | Data Source | Green | Yellow | Red |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-------|--------|-----|
| Usage Frequency | 25% | Shipments/week | >5 | 2-5 | <2 |
| Feature Adoption | 20% | Capabilities used | >3 features | 2 features | 1 feature |
| User Engagement | 20% | Portal logins | Weekly | Bi-weekly | <Monthly |
| Support Tickets | 15% | Issues/month | 0-1 | 2-3 | >3 |
| Support Tickets | 15% | Response time | <4 hours | 4-24h | >24h |
| NPS/CSAT | 10% | Last survey | >8 | 6-7 | <6 |

**Health Score Actions:**

| Score | Status | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 80-100 | 🟢 Healthy | Monthly check-ins, expansion focus |
| 60-79 | 🟡 At Risk | Bi-weekly touchpoints, identify blockers |
| <60 | 🔴 Critical | Weekly calls, executive escalation plan |

### Proactive Health Interventions

**The "Usage Drop" Alert:**
- **Trigger:** Shipments drop 50% vs. prior 30-day average
- **Action:** Same-day outreach — "Noticed volume down, everything okay?"
- **Common Causes:** Seasonal slowdown, internal process change, competitor pilot, stakeholder departure
- **Discovery Questions:**
  - "Has anything changed in your shipping patterns?"
  - "Are you still the right person for supply chain visibility?"
  - "Any challenges with the platform I can help with?"

**The "Feature Stagnation" Alert:**
- **Trigger:** Using only basic tracking after 60 days
- **Action:** Training offer — "Let's explore features that save time"
- **Opportunity:** Demonstrate unused capabilities (alerts, reporting, integrations)

## 4. Playbook: Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)

### QBR Structure: The 6-Slide Framework

**Slide 1: Executive Summary**
- Health score trend (3 quarters)
- One-sentence status: "On track," "Needs attention," or "At risk"
- Recommended action(s)

**Slide 2: Usage & Adoption Metrics**
- Shipments tracked (trending graph)
- Active users (growth/churn)
- Feature utilization heatmap

**Slide 3: Value Delivered**
- Incidents detected (with $ impact if known)
- Claims prevented / disputes resolved
- Time saved vs. manual tracking
- Customer quote(s) on value received

**Slide 4: Operational Excellence**
- Uptime / reliability metrics
- Alert accuracy (true positives vs. false alarms)
- Support response times
- Any service issues + resolution

**Slide 5: Expansion Opportunities**
- New lanes identified
- Additional use cases (asset tracking, vehicle tracking)
- Integration opportunities (TMS, ERP)
- Volume growth projections

**Slide 6: Next Quarter Roadmap**
- Customer goals for next 90 days
- Decklar commitments (training, features, support)
- Joint success metrics
- Meeting schedule locked

### Proactive QBR Preparation

**2 Weeks Before:**
- Pull usage data, identify trends
- Review call history for discussion points
- Draft preliminary opportunity list

**1 Week Before:**
- Send agenda + data preview to customer
- Request: "What would make this QBR most valuable?"
- Prepare expansion proposal if applicable

**Day Before:**
- Confirm attendees (stakeholder changes = risk signal)
- Test screen share, backup materials ready
- Review competitor landscape (any new threats?)

**Day After:**
- Send summary within 24 hours
- Update memory with all commitments
- Schedule follow-up actions

## 5. Playbook: Expansion & Upsell

### The Expansion Signal Matrix

| Signal | Interpretation | Approach |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| Adding new lanes | Growth mode | "Let's discuss volume pricing" |
| Asking about APIs | Integration need | "Our enterprise tier includes API access" |
| Mentioning competitors | Evaluating alternatives | "Can you share what's missing?" |
| User count growing | Team adoption | "Multi-user features you're not using" |
| Seasonal volume spike | Temporary need | "Flexible contract for peak season" |
| Compliance questions | Regulatory pressure | "Pharma-grade features available" |

### The Expansion Conversation Framework

**Phase 1: Discovery (Not Pitching)**
- "What's your biggest supply chain visibility challenge right now?"
- "Are there shipments you're NOT tracking that you'd like to?"
- "Who else in your organization needs this data?"
- "What would make Decklar indispensable?"

**Phase 2: Value Mapping**
- Connect their stated need to Decklar capability
- Reference similar customer success story
- Rough ROI calculation: "If we prevented one claim per month..."

**Phase 3: Proposal**
- Specific recommendation with clear pricing
- Pilot option if risk-averse customer
- Timeline: "We could have this live by [date]"

**Phase 4: Close**
- Clear next step with date
- Written follow-up within 2 hours
- Update memory with opportunity details

## 6. Playbook: At-Risk Customer Recovery

### Early Warning Signs

| Sign | Detection | Response Time |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Usage decline | Weekly data review | 24 hours |
| Support ticket mentions competitor | Ticket text | Immediate |
| Stakeholder departure | LinkedIn, customer mention | 48 hours |
| Missed meetings | Calendar | Same day |
| Late payments | Finance alert | 1 week |
| NPS detractor score | Survey | 24 hours |

### The Recovery Protocol

**Step 1: Root Cause Diagnosis (Before Outreach)**
- Review full call history — any unresolved issues?
- Check support tickets — any recurring problems?
- Analyze usage data — specific drop-off point?
- Research customer — acquisitions, leadership changes, financial news?

**Step 2: Strategic Outreach**
- Skip the "checking in" email — be direct
- Example: "I noticed [specific metric] changed. I'm concerned we may not be delivering value. Can we talk about what's changed?"
- Offer specific value: training, executive briefing, custom report

**Step 3: The Recovery Call**
- Acknowledge the gap directly
- Ask: "What would need to change for Decklar to be essential?"
- Listen more than talk
- Commit to specific fixes with dates
- Schedule follow-up before call ends

**Step 4: Recovery Execution**
- Update memory with recovery plan
- Execute all commitments on time or early
- Weekly touchpoints until health score improves
- Escalate to Decklar leadership if at-risk >30 days

## 7. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) for Jeff

### Individual Account Metrics
- **Time to First Value:** Days from kickoff to first tracked shipment
- **Adoption Rate:** % of provisioned users who are active weekly
- **Health Score Trend:** Direction over 90 days
- **Expansion Revenue:** Upsell/cross-sell dollars per account

### Portfolio Metrics
- **Logo Retention Rate:** % customers renewing annually
- **Net Revenue Retention:** $ renewal + expansion - contraction
- **At-Risk Rate:** % accounts red/yellow health
- **QBR Completion:** % accounts with quarterly review completed

### Leading Indicators (Predictive)
- **Engagement Velocity:** Login frequency in first 30 days
- **Feature Breadth:** # capabilities used by Day 60
- **Support Interaction Quality:** Resolution satisfaction scores
- **Stakeholder Relationship Count:** # champions identified
