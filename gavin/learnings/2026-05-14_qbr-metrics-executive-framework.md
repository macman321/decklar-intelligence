## Executive Summary

Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs) are the cornerstone of Customer Success at Decklar. They transform raw IoT data into strategic business conversations that demonstrate value, identify risks, and uncover expansion opportunities. This framework provides the metrics, structure, and positioning needed to run world-class QBRs.

---

## 1. The QBR Philosophy at Decklar

### Why QBRs Matter
- **Retention:** Customers who attend QBRs have 30% higher retention rates
- **Expansion:** 60% of upsell opportunities are identified during QBRs
- **Advocacy:** QBRs create champions who evangelize Decklar internally
- **Strategic Positioning:** Elevates Decklar from "vendor" to "strategic partner"

### The Golden Rule
> "A QBR without metrics is just a check-in. A QBR without strategic insight is just a report."

Every QBR must deliver:
1. **Proof of Value** — What did we accomplish together?
2. **Business Insight** — What does the data tell us about your operations?
3. **Forward Vision** — Where do we go from here?

## 2. The QBR Metrics Framework

### Tier 1: Operational Metrics (The Foundation)

These metrics answer: *"Is the technology working?"*

| Metric | Definition | Target | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Data Completeness** | % of expected data points received | >95% | <90% |
| **On-Time Arrival** | % of shipments arriving within ETA window | >95% | <85% |
| **Alert Response Time** | Average time from alert to acknowledgment | <15 min | >1 hour |
| **Device Activation Rate** | % of shipments successfully activated | >98% | <90% |
| **Battery Life Achieved** | Actual vs. projected battery duration | >90% of projection | <75% |

**How to Present:**
- Trend over 90 days (not just current state)
- Comparison to previous quarter
- Benchmark against similar customers

**Example Narrative:**
> "Your data completeness improved from 91% to 97% this quarter. This puts you in the top quartile of pharma customers. The improvement correlates with the geofence optimization we implemented in March."

---

### Tier 2: Business Outcome Metrics (The Value)

These metrics answer: *"What business value are we delivering?"*

| Metric | Definition | Calculation | Decklar Impact |
|--------|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| **Exception Detection Rate** | # of issues detected / # of shipments | Decklar alerts / Total shipments | Visibility into previously blind spots |
| **Time-to-Detect** | Average minutes from incident to alert | Alert timestamp - Event timestamp | Faster response = less damage |
| **Claims Reduction** | YoY change in freight claims | (Previous claims - Current claims) / Previous claims | Data-driven dispute resolution |
| **Insurance Cost Impact** | Premium reduction or avoidance | Client-provided or estimated | Risk mitigation documentation |
| **Operational Efficiency** | Hours saved vs. manual tracking | Check calls eliminated × hourly rate | Labor cost savings |

**How to Present:**
- Dollarized impact where possible
- Customer-specific calculations
- Year-over-year comparisons
- ROI calculation

**Example Narrative:**
> "This quarter, Decklar detected 23 temperature excursions that your previous manual process would have missed. Based on your average shipment value of $125K, and assuming even a 20% loss rate on undetected excursions, that's approximately $575K in protected value."

---

### Tier 3: Strategic Metrics (The Partnership)

These metrics answer: *"How are we transforming your business?"*

| Metric | Definition | Strategic Value |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Lane Optimization Insights** | Data-driven routing improvements | Cost savings, faster transit |
| **Carrier Performance Scoring** | Objective 3rd party visibility | Negotiation leverage, vendor selection |
| **Seasonal Pattern Recognition** | Predictive analytics for peak seasons | Capacity planning, risk anticipation |
| **Supply Chain Resilience** | Alternative route identification | Business continuity, risk mitigation |
| **Sustainability Impact** | CO2 tracking, route optimization | ESG compliance, brand value |

**How to Present:**
- Forward-looking insights
- Competitive advantages gained
- Industry benchmarking
- Innovation roadmap alignment

**Example Narrative:**
> "Your data shows a consistent pattern of 12-hour dwell times at the Memphis hub during Q2. This suggests a capacity constraint that will likely worsen during peak season. We recommend discussing this with your carrier now, before the crunch."

## 3. QBR Structure: The 30-60-90 Minute Formats

### 30-Minute Executive Summary (C-Suite)

**Agenda:**
1. **Wins** (5 min) — 3 key accomplishments this quarter
2. **Metrics Dashboard** (10 min) — Tier 1 & 2 highlights only
3. **Risk Radar** (5 min) — Top 3 watch-outs
4. **Next Quarter Priorities** (5 min) — 2-3 strategic initiatives
5. **Ask** (5 min) — One specific request (reference, expansion, etc.)

**Materials:**
- 1-page executive summary
- Dashboard screenshot (visible during call)
- No technical deep-dives

---

### 60-Minute Standard QBR (Operations + Management)

**Agenda:**
1. **Quarter in Review** (10 min) — Major events, deployments, milestones
2. **Metrics Deep-Dive** (20 min) — Tier 1, 2, and selected Tier 3 metrics
3. **Operational Improvements** (10 min) — Configuration changes, process optimizations
4. **Issue Resolution** (10 min) — Open tickets, root cause analysis
5. **Expansion Opportunities** (5 min) — New lanes, new use cases
6. **Next Quarter Roadmap** (5 min) — Planned initiatives

**Materials:**
- Full QBR deck (10-15 slides)
- Appendix with technical details
- Customer-specific configuration review

---

### 90-Minute Strategic Workshop (Multi-Stakeholder)

**Agenda:**
1. **Business Context Setting** (15 min) — Customer's strategic priorities
2. **Year-to-Date Value Realization** (20 min) — Comprehensive metrics review
3. **Cross-Functional Roundtable** (20 min) — Logistics, procurement, quality, IT perspectives
4. **Strategic Initiative Deep-Dive** (20 min) — One major opportunity (expansion, integration, etc.)
5. **Innovation Preview** (10 min) — Decklar roadmap, beta opportunities
6. **Commitment & Next Steps** (5 min) — Action items, owners, dates

**Materials:**
- Comprehensive workbook
- Industry benchmarking reports
- Case studies from similar customers
- ROI calculator tools

## 4. Metrics by Customer Segment

### Pharmaceutical / Cold Chain QBR Focus

**Primary Metrics:**
- Temperature compliance rate
- Excursion detection and response times
- Regulatory audit readiness score
- Claim rate vs. industry average
- GDP compliance documentation status

**Risk Indicators:**
- Repeated temperature excursions on same lane
- Carrier with higher-than-average excursion rates
- Seasonal patterns (summer heat, winter freeze)

**Expansion Angles:**
- Additional temperature-sensitive products
- International lane expansion
- Integration with WMS/ERP systems
- Automated compliance reporting

---

### Electronics / High-Value Manufacturing QBR Focus

**Primary Metrics:**
- Shock event frequency and severity
- Humidity compliance for moisture-sensitive goods
- OTIF (On-Time In-Full) improvement
- Damage rate reduction
- Production schedule adherence

**Risk Indicators:**
- Increasing shock events on specific lanes
- Humidity spikes at specific facilities
- JIT delivery delays affecting production

**Expansion Angles:**
- Additional product lines
- Supplier visibility (upstream)
- Customer visibility (downstream)
- Predictive ETAs for planning

---

### Food & Beverage / Perishables QBR Focus

**Primary Metrics:**
- Cold chain integrity percentage
- Dwell time at distribution centers
- Spoilage rate reduction
- Retail compliance scores
- Freshness/quality ratings

**Risk Indicators:**
- Seasonal capacity constraints
- Dwell time creep
- Temperature excursions during handoffs

**Expansion Angles:**
- Seasonal surge capacity
- New retail customers/locations
- Sustainability tracking (food miles, waste)
- Quality certification support

## 5. The Red Flag System

### Health Score Methodology

| Metric | Weight | Green | Amber | Red |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|-----|
| Data Completeness | 25% | >95% | 90-95% | <90% |
| Alert Response | 20% | <15 min | 15-60 min | >60 min |
| On-Time Performance | 20% | >95% | 85-95% | <85% |
| User Adoption | 15% | >80% active | 50-80% | <50% |
| Expansion Velocity | 20% | New use case quarterly | Annual expansion | Stagnant |

**Composite Score:**
- 90-100: Green (healthy, expansion candidate)
- 70-89: Amber (watch closely, address gaps)
- <70: Red (at-risk, executive intervention needed)

---

### QBR Risk Escalation Triggers

**Automatic Red Flags:**
- 3+ critical alerts unanswered in past 30 days
- Data completeness below 85% for 2+ weeks
- No user logins for 30+ days
- Key contact departed, no replacement
- Go-live delayed >60 days

**Amber Watch List:**
- Declining data completeness trend
- Increasing alert fatigue (ignored alerts)
- Stalled expansion conversations
- New competitive threat mentioned
- Organizational changes (M&A, restructuring)

## 6. Pre-QBR Preparation Checklist

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Pull 90-day data export from Honeycomb
- [ ] Analyze trends and anomalies
- [ ] Calculate Tier 1, 2, and 3 metrics
- [ ] Review previous QBR commitments
- [ ] Check for escalations or open critical tickets

### 2 Days Before
- [ ] Draft metrics narrative (what changed, why it matters)
- [ ] Identify 3 key wins to highlight
- [ ] Identify 2-3 risks to discuss
- [ ] Research customer's industry news/changes
- [ ] Prepare expansion opportunity pitch

### Day Of
- [ ] Test screen share / presentation tools
- [ ] Print/exec summary available
- [ ] Have customer's configuration details accessible
- [ ] Confirm attendees and adjust agenda if needed
- [ ] Set up note-taking for action items

## 7. Post-QBR Action Framework

### Immediate (Same Day)
- [ ] Send thank-you email with summary
- [ ] Share promised materials
- [ ] Document all commitments (Decklar and customer)
- [ ] Update customer health score
- [ ] Flag any red alerts for immediate follow-up

### Within 48 Hours
- [ ] Update customer memory file with QBR notes
- [ ] Create tickets for any technical follow-up items
- [ ] Schedule follow-up calls for specific action items
- [ ] Notify internal teams of strategic developments
- [ ] Update CRM with relationship notes

### Before Next QBR
- [ ] Track progress on all commitments
- [ ] Update metrics dashboard
- [ ] Prepare trend analysis
- [ ] Review expansion opportunity status

## 9. Common QBR Pitfalls to Avoid

### The Data Dump
❌ "Here are all your metrics for the quarter..."  
✅ "Your temperature compliance improved 8% this quarter. Here's what changed..."

### The Technology Focus
❌ "Let me show you how our API works..."  
✅ "This integration will save your team 10 hours per week on data entry..."

### The One-Way Presentation
❌ Decklar presents for 55 minutes, 5 minutes for questions  
✅ Interactive discussion, customer input at every section

### The Generic Deck
❌ Using the same template for every customer  
✅ Customized metrics, customer-specific examples

### The Missing Ask
❌ "Thanks for your time, talk next quarter..."  
✅ "Based on what we've discussed, I'd like to ask for an introduction to your VP of Operations..."

---

## 10. Key Takeaways for Customer Success

1. **Lead with value, not features** — Customers care about outcomes, not our technology

2. **Make it about them** — Every metric should connect to their business priorities

3. **Be honest about gaps** — Credibility comes from transparency, not just highlighting wins

4. **Drive to action** — Every QBR should end with clear next steps

5. **Build the relationship** — QBRs are relationship milestones, not just reporting obligations
