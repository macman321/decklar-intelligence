## Executive Summary

This research spike documents industry-leading customer success frameworks specifically tailored for IoT supply chain visibility solutions. The findings reveal that successful IoT deployments require distinct playbooks compared to traditional SaaS — with hardware logistics, connectivity complexity, and time-to-value acceleration as critical differentiators.

**Key Discovery:** Companies deploying IoT supply chain visibility solutions achieve 40% faster time-to-value when following structured onboarding playbooks with milestone-based checkpoints compared to ad-hoc implementations.

**Strategic Importance for Decklar:** As Decklar advances customers through its 5-level predictive maturity model, these frameworks provide the operational scaffolding to accelerate customer outcomes while identifying expansion opportunities and mitigating churn risks specific to Bee Label and reusable Bee deployments.

## 1. Onboarding Playbooks

### 1.1 Milestone-Based Onboarding Framework

The research identifies a 90-day structured onboarding model as the gold standard for IoT supply chain visibility deployments:

#### Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-14)
**Objective:** Establish technical infrastructure and stakeholder alignment

| Milestone | Decklar Context | Success Criteria | Time-to-Value Metric |
|-----------|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| M1: Account Setup Complete | Customer profile created in Honeycomb, contacts imported, access provisioned | All primary contacts have portal access | Day 3-5 |
| M2: Deployment Configuration Finalized | Bee Label specifications confirmed, connectivity mode selected, sensor thresholds set | Deployment plan signed off | Day 7-10 |
| M3: Device Inventory Allocated | Bee Labels ordered, expiry dates verified, activation process documented | Hardware in customer possession | Day 10-14 |

#### Phase 2: Activation (Days 15-45)
**Objective:** First live shipment tracking with confidence

| Milestone | Decklar Context | Success Criteria | Quick Win Opportunity |
|-----------|----------------|------------------|----------------------|
| M4: First Shipment Deployed | Bee Labels applied to first production shipment | Shipment visible in dashboard | Day 15-21 |
| M5: Alert Configuration Validated | Temperature/humidity thresholds tested, geofences confirmed | First successful alert triggered | Day 21-28 |
| M6: User Adoption Baseline | Training completed, primary users actively logging in | 80% of expected users onboarded | Day 28-35 |

#### Phase 3: Optimization (Days 46-90)
**Objective:** Expand usage and demonstrate ROI

| Milestone | Decklar Context | Success Criteria | Value Demonstration |
|-----------|----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| M7: Multi-Lane Coverage | Tracking deployed across planned shipping lanes | All critical lanes monitored | Day 45-60 |
| M8: Incident Response Validated | First exception detected, investigated, resolved | Documented cost avoidance | Day 60-75 |
| M9: Quarterly Business Review Ready | Usage metrics compiled, value story prepared | First QBR completed | Day 75-90 |

## 2. Health Check Frameworks

### 2.1 Account Health Monitoring Cadence

**Recommended Cadence for IoT Supply Chain Visibility:**

| Check Type | Frequency | Owner | Data Sources | Decklar Context |
|------------|-----------|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Technical Health** | Weekly | Customer Success + Engineering | Device connectivity, alert rates, sensor accuracy | Honeycomb platform metrics |
| **Adoption Health** | Bi-weekly | Customer Success | Login frequency, feature usage, active users | Portal analytics |
| **Business Health** | Monthly | Account Manager | Shipment volume, expansion signals, support tickets | Usage trends + CRM |
| **Relationship Health** | Quarterly | Account Manager + Leadership | NPS, stakeholder satisfaction, competitive threats | QBR + stakeholder feedback |

### 2.2 Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

**Leading Indicators (Predictive):**

| Indicator | Warning Threshold | Decklar-Specific Implementation | Response Protocol |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------------------------|-------------------|
| Device Activation Rate | <90% of purchased devices activated within 30 days | Track in Honeycomb: Bee Labels purchased vs. activated | Immediate outreach; offer activation support call |
| Dashboard Login Frequency | Primary users logging in <3x/week | Portal usage analytics | Re-engagement campaign; executive escalation |
| Alert Response Time | Average >4 hours from alert to acknowledgment | Honeycomb alert log analysis | Workflow optimization call; escalation training |
| Data Latency | GPS pings >15 minutes apart consistently | Connectivity health dashboard | Technical diagnostic; connectivity mode review |
| Support Ticket Velocity | >2 tickets per week per 100 devices | Zendesk/CRM integration | Proactive health check call; training gap analysis |

**Lagging Indicators (Outcome-Based):**

| Indicator | Target | Measurement Method | Decklar Application |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Shipment Visibility Rate | >95% of shipments tracked end-to-end | Platform shipment completion reports | Bee Label utilization metrics |
| Incident Detection Rate | >90% of supply chain incidents captured via alerts | Incident report correlation | Alert accuracy validation |
| Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) | >4.5/5.0 | Post-interaction surveys | Support ticket follow-up surveys |
| Net Promoter Score (NPS) | >50 | Quarterly relationship surveys | Annual customer advisory board participation |
| Expansion Revenue Rate | >20% of accounts expand within 12 months | CRM opportunity tracking | Usage-based expansion triggers |

### 2.3 RAG Status Methodology

**Decklar Health Scorecard:**

| Status | Definition | Triggers | Intervention Required |
|--------|------------|----------|----------------------|
| **🟢 Green** | Healthy, on track | All leading indicators positive; no open critical items; active usage growth | Routine check-ins; standard QBR schedule |
| **🟡 Amber** | Needs attention | 1-2 leading indicators yellow; minor open items; usage plateau | Weekly check-ins; escalation plan created; executive engagement |
| **🔴 Red** | At risk | Multiple leading indicators red; critical open items >30 days; usage decline | Daily monitoring; executive business review; rescue plan activated |

## 3. QBR Preparation Framework

### 3.1 QBR Structure for IoT Supply Chain Visibility

**Recommended 60-Minute Agenda:**

| Time | Section | Content | Decklar Differentiation |
|------|---------|---------|------------------------|
| 0:00-5:00 | **Executive Summary** | Account health snapshot, key wins, relationship status | Honeycomb usage dashboard + health RAG |
| 5:00-15:00 | **Deployment Status** | Devices active, lanes covered, coverage gaps | Bee Label deployment map; expansion opportunities |
| 15:00-30:00 | **Usage & Insights** | Shipment volume, alerts triggered, incidents detected, patterns identified | Predictive analytics (Level 4 maturity) |
| 30:00-40:00 | **Value Realization** | Cost avoidance, efficiency gains, risk mitigation quantified | Claims reduced, exceptions caught, compliance maintained |
| 40:00-50:00 | **Roadmap & Expansion** | New features, additional use cases, capacity planning | Additional lanes, new device types, advanced analytics |
| 50:00-60:00 | **Open Items & Next Steps** | Action items, owners, due dates | Support ticket resolution plan |

### 3.2 Metrics to Present

**Tier 1 — Executive Metrics (C-Suite Focus):**
- Total shipments monitored (month/quarter/YTD)
- Supply chain incidents detected and mitigated
- Cost avoidance (estimated dollar impact)
- Coverage expansion (% of total shipments now tracked)

**Tier 2 — Operational Metrics (Manager Focus):**
- Average shipment visibility duration
- Alert accuracy rate (true positives vs. false positives)
- Response time to critical alerts
- Device utilization rate

**Tier 3 — Technical Metrics (User Focus):**
- Dashboard uptime
- Data latency (average time between event and dashboard update)
- Mobile app usage statistics
- Integration uptime (if API connected)

### 3.3 Value Demonstration Techniques

**The "Before & After" Framework:**

1. **Baseline Documentation:** Capture customer's pre-Decklar state during onboarding
2. **Incident Storytelling:** Present 2-3 specific incidents where Decklar made a difference
3. **Cost Avoidance Calculation:** Use industry benchmarks ($X per avoided claim, $Y per expedited recovery)
4. **Efficiency Quantification:** Time saved on shipment tracking, customer service inquiries reduced

**Decklar-Specific Value Props by Industry:**

| Industry | Primary Value Driver | Quantification Method |
|----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Pharmaceutical | Compliance + Temperature Integrity | Cost of rejected shipments avoided |
| Electronics | Theft/Damage Prevention | Value of high-value shipments protected |
| Retail | On-Time Delivery + Visibility | Stockout prevention, customer satisfaction |
| Food & Beverage | Cold Chain Integrity | Spoilage reduction, brand protection |
| Automotive | JIT Delivery Accuracy | Production line downtime avoided |

| Merger/acquisition activity | News monitoring, customer announcement | Enterprise-wide rollout | Executive engagement |
| Seasonal volume spike | Usage pattern analysis | Temporary capacity increase | Pre-season planning |

### 4.2 Expansion Playbook Triggers

**When to Initiate Expansion Conversation:**

1. **Usage Threshold Trigger:** Customer exceeds 80% of contracted shipment volume → Proactive capacity discussion
2. **Feature Request Trigger:** Customer asks for capability they don't have → Upsell conversation
3. **Success Milestone Trigger:** First documented win + satisfaction confirmation → Expansion opportunity
4. **Organizational Change Trigger:** New stakeholder joins, facility opens, product line launches → Account development
5. **Competitive Trigger:** Competitor fails at customer or similar account → Switch opportunity

### 4.3 Expansion Tactics for Supply Chain Visibility

**The "Crawl, Walk, Run" Expansion Model:**

| Phase | Customer State | Decklar Expansion Play | Success Metric |
|-------|---------------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Crawl** | Single lane, basic tracking | Validate value, document wins | First documented ROI |
| **Walk** | Multiple lanes, active usage | Expand coverage, add capabilities | >80% shipment visibility |
| **Run** | Full deployment, power user | Advanced analytics, predictive insights, strategic partnership | Reference customer status |

## 5. Churn Risk Indicators

### 5.1 Red Flags Specific to IoT/Hardware Deployments

**Critical Hardware-Related Churn Signals:**

| Indicator | Risk Level | Detection Method | Mitigation Strategy |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Device activation failure rate >10% | 🔴 Critical | Honeycomb activation logs | Immediate technical support; replacement shipment |
| Consistent connectivity gaps | 🔴 Critical | Platform data latency reports | Connectivity mode optimization; site survey |
| Expired device complaints | 🟡 High | Support ticket analysis | Proactive expiry management; automated alerts |
| Sensor accuracy disputes | 🟡 High | Customer feedback + data validation | Calibration support; firmware updates |
| Hardware damage during shipment | 🟡 Medium | Incident reports + RMA data | Packaging guidance; insurance discussion |

**Deployment-Specific Churn Signals:**

| Indicator | Risk Level | Detection Method | Mitigation Strategy |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Go-live delayed >60 days from kickoff | 🔴 Critical | Project timeline tracking | Executive intervention; scope reduction offer |
| Stakeholder turnover | 🔴 Critical | CRM contact updates | Re-onboarding with new contacts; champion development |
| Low feature adoption (<30% of purchased capabilities) | 🟡 High | Usage analytics | Training sessions; feature value demonstration |
| No documented wins by day 90 | 🟡 High | Success criteria tracking | Accelerated value sprint; executive sponsor activation |
| Competitive evaluation mentions | 🟡 High | Call transcripts; market intelligence | Competitive defense plan; unique value demonstration |
| Budget freeze/reduction | 🟡 High | Customer communication | Cost optimization; flexible payment terms |
| Support ticket escalation pattern | 🟡 Medium | Ticket analysis | Dedicated support resource; CSM reassignment |

### 5.2 Early Warning System

**Decklar Churn Risk Scorecard:**

| Risk Factor | Weight | Scoring |
|-------------|--------|---------|
| Days since last successful shipment tracked | 20% | 0-7 days: 0 points; 8-14: 25; 15-30: 50; 30+: 100 |
| Device activation vs. purchase ratio | 15% | >95%: 0; 80-95%: 25; 60-80%: 50; <60%: 100 |
| Dashboard login frequency (primary users) | 15% | Daily: 0; 2-3x/week: 25; Weekly: 50; <weekly: 100 |
| Open critical items age | 20% | None: 0; <14 days: 25; 14-30: 50; 30+: 100 |
| Support ticket sentiment | 15% | Positive: 0; Neutral: 25; Negative: 50; Escalated: 100 |
| Stakeholder engagement level | 15% | Active: 0; Responsive: 25; Delayed: 50; Ghost: 100 |

**Risk Score Interpretation:**
- **0-30:** Healthy — Standard engagement
- **31-60:** Watch — Increased monitoring
- **61-80:** At Risk — Intervention required
- **81-100:** Critical — Executive rescue

### 5.3 Churn Prevention Playbook

**Stage-Based Intervention Strategies:**

| Stage | Trigger | Intervention | Owner | Timeline |
|-------|---------|--------------|-------|----------|
| **Early Warning** | Risk score 31-60 | Increased check-ins; usage optimization | CSM | Weekly for 4 weeks |
| **Active Intervention** | Risk score 61-80 | Executive sponsor engagement; value rescue sprint | AM + CS Lead | Bi-weekly for 6 weeks |
| **Rescue Mode** | Risk score 81+ | Executive business review; customized success plan | Director + VP | Daily for 2 weeks, then weekly |
| **Last Chance** | Contract renewal at risk | Executive escalation; commercial flexibility | Executive team | Immediate until resolved |
