## Executive Summary

Every customer conversation should include quantifiable value. Decklar's IoT sensors generate **three categories of measurable ROI**:

1. **Loss Prevention** — Avoided write-offs, claims, and penalties
2. **Operational Efficiency** — Reduced labor, faster decisions, optimized processes
3. **Insurance & Compliance** — Premium reductions, audit readiness, regulatory adherence

The key is matching the right ROI framework to the customer's industry and use case.

## 2. ROI Calculator Framework

### The "3-30-300" Rule for Customer Conversations

When quantifying value, use this framework:

```
3%   → Hard savings (direct cost avoidance)
30%  → Soft savings (efficiency, time, labor)
300% → Strategic value (customer satisfaction, competitive advantage)
```

**Example:** A pharma customer with $10M annual cold chain spend:
- **Hard savings:** $300K (3% excursion prevention)
- **Soft savings:** $3M (operational efficiency, faster decisions)
- **Strategic value:** $30M (brand protection, patient safety, competitive positioning)

### ROI Formula Template

```
Annual ROI = (Losses Prevented + Efficiency Gains + Compliance Value) - Decklar Cost
           ___________________________________________________________________
                             Decklar Cost

Break-even: Typically 2–6 months for high-volume shippers
Year 1 ROI: Typically 300–800% for enterprise customers
```

## 3. Customer Conversation Framework

### Discovery Questions for ROI Quantification

**Phase 1: Current State Assessment**
1. "What's your annual spend on [cargo type] that could be at risk?"
2. "How many temperature excursions/write-offs did you have last year?"
3. "What's your average shipment value?"
4. "How much do you spend on expedited replacements annually?"
5. "What are your insurance premiums for [cargo type]?"

**Phase 2: Pain Point Valuation**
1. "When a shipment is compromised, what's the total cost beyond the product itself?"
2. "How many hours does your team spend tracking down shipment status?"
3. "What does a rejected load cost you in lost revenue + disposal + replacement?"
4. "How much have you paid in detention/demurrage fees in the last 12 months?"

**Phase 3: Value Hypothesis**
1. "Based on similar customers, we typically see [X%] reduction in [metric]. Applied to your volume, that would save approximately $[amount]."
2. "If we could give you real-time visibility that prevented just [one/two] incidents per quarter, what would that be worth?"

## 8. Key Insights & Best Practices

### Insight 1: Always Lead with the Customer's Numbers
Generic benchmarks are useful, but **customer-specific calculations** close deals. Ask for their data and build the ROI case together.

### Insight 2: The "One Incident" Narrative is Powerful
For high-value cargo, the story is simple: "One prevented incident pays for the program." This is intellectually easy and emotionally compelling.

### Insight 3: Soft Savings Matter
Don't discount operational efficiency, customer satisfaction, and brand protection. These are harder to quantify but often exceed hard savings in total value.

### Insight 4: Document Everything
Track actual ROI for existing customers. Build a library of real case studies with verified numbers. Nothing beats "Customer X saved $Y."

### Insight 5: Revisit ROI Quarterly
Customer operations change. New regulations emerge. Revisit the ROI conversation quarterly to identify new value opportunities.
