## Executive Summary

Effective customer conversations follow a progression: **Discovery → Diagnosis → Prescription → Proof → Partnership**. This learning captures proven conversation frameworks for every stage of the Decklar customer lifecycle. These aren't scripts—they're frameworks that adapt to customer context while ensuring key elements are covered.

## 1. The Discovery Call Framework (First Engagement)

### Objective
Uncover the customer's true pain point—not what they think they want, but what they actually need. Most customers describe symptoms ("we need tracking"), not root causes ("we're losing $2M annually to disputed claims").

### The 5-Question Discovery Sequence

**Question 1: The Pain Point**
> "What's your biggest supply chain visibility challenge right now?"

*What to listen for:*
- Do they mention specific dollar amounts? (Strong buying signal)
- Do they describe a recent incident? (Urgency indicator)
- Are they vague? (Early stage, needs education)

*Probe deeper:*
- "How often does that happen?"
- "What's the impact when it does?"

**Question 2: The Current State**
> "How are you handling that today?"

*What to listen for:*
- Manual processes = opportunity
- Competitor mention = evaluation mode
- "Nothing" = greenfield opportunity

*Probe deeper:*
- "What works about that approach?"
- "What's the limitation you keep hitting?"

**Question 3: The Consequence**
> "What happens if this isn't solved in the next 6 months?"

*What to listen for:*
- Specific business impact (budget, customers, risk)
- Personal impact (their job, their reputation)
- Lack of urgency = not a priority right now

*Probe deeper:*
- "Has this ever escalated to your leadership?"
- "What's the most expensive incident you've had?"

**Question 4: The Ideal Future**
> "If you had perfect visibility, what would change?"

*What to listen for:*
- ROI language (time saved, claims prevented)
- Integration points (systems, partners)
- Success metrics (how they'd measure value)

*Probe deeper:*
- "How would you measure success?"
- "Who else would benefit from that visibility?"

**Question 5: The Decision Process**
> "Assuming we can solve this, what would the decision process look like?"

*What to listen for:*
- Other stakeholders involved
- Budget authority and process
- Timeline expectations

*Probe deeper:*
- "What's typically required to get something like this approved?"
- "Who else should be part of this conversation?"

### Discovery Red Flags

| Red Flag | Interpretation | Response |
|----------|----------------|----------|
| "Just send me pricing" | Price shopping, not value-focused | "Of course. First, can you share what problem you're solving so I can point you to the right tier?" |
| "We're just exploring" | No urgency, no budget | "Totally understand. What would need to happen to make this a priority?" |
| "Can you talk to [junior person]?" | No decision maker access | "Happy to. I'll also want to understand your specific goals—are you the right person for that conversation?" |
| "We need to start tomorrow" | Unrealistic expectations | "We can move fast, but I'd rather get you set up right than fast. Here's what's actually needed..." |

## 2. The Value Proposition Framework

### The 3-Layer Value Stack

When presenting Decklar, structure the value in three layers:

**Layer 1: Operational Value (Immediate)**
> "You know where every shipment is, right now, without calling drivers or carriers."

*Proof points:*
- Real-time GPS tracking
- Automated alerts
- Mobile dashboard access

**Layer 2: Financial Value (30-60 days)**
> "You prevent claims before they happen, and when disputes arise, you have proof on your side."

*Proof points:*
- Incident detection before delivery
- Data-backed dispute resolution
- Insurance premium reductions (in some cases)

**Layer 3: Strategic Value (90+ days)**
> "You optimize your entire supply chain based on actual performance data, not carrier promises."

*Proof points:*
- Lane performance analytics
- Carrier scorecards
- Predictive delay alerts

### Tailoring by Industry

| Industry | Lead with... | Proof Point |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Food & Beverage | Temperature compliance | "Cold chain violations cost an average of $15K per incident" |
| Pharma | Regulatory requirements | "21 CFR Part 11 compliance built in" |
| Retail | Customer experience | "Proactive delay notifications before customers call" |
| Manufacturing | JIT delivery | "Production-line-down prevention" |
| Freight Brokerage | Carrier accountability | "Proof of performance for your customers" |

### The ROI Conversation

**Never lead with ROI calculations.** Customers are skeptical of vendor-provided ROI. Instead:

1. **Ask for their numbers:** "What does a typical claim cost you?"
2. **Let them calculate:** "If we prevented 5 of those per quarter, what would that mean?"
3. **Anchor conservatively:** "Most customers see 2-3 incidents caught in the first 60 days."

## 3. The Objection Handling Framework

### The Acknowledge → Reframe → Validate → Pivot Method

**Common Objection: "It's too expensive"**

*Acknowledge:*
> "I hear that a lot, and budget constraints are real."

*Reframe:*
> "Let me ask—what's the cost of NOT having visibility? You mentioned disputed claims..."

*Validate:*
> "You're right that there's an investment here."

*Pivot:*
> "Many customers start with a pilot on their highest-risk lanes. That keeps initial cost low while proving value. Would that work for you?"

**Common Objection: "We're already working with [Competitor]"**

*Acknowledge:*
> "That makes sense—they're a known player."

*Reframe:*
> "Most of our customers came from [Competitor]. What they found was [specific limitation]. Are you seeing that?"

*Validate:*
> "Switching vendors is a real consideration."

*Pivot:*
> "Would it be worth a 20-minute conversation to understand what you might be missing? No commitment—we'll even share what we learn about your current setup."

**Common Objection: "We don't have time to implement"**

*Acknowledge:*
> "Time is your scarcest resource."

*Reframe:*
> "Our fastest customers are live in 72 hours. What makes it fast is we handle the heavy lifting—just need 30 minutes of your time for kickoff."

*Validate:*
> "Implementation overhead is a legitimate concern."

*Pivot:*
> "Let me walk you through exactly what you'd need to do. Then you can decide if it fits your timeline."

### The Silence Rule

After handling an objection, **stop talking**. The next person to speak loses. Count to 8 in your head if needed.

## 4. The Kickoff Conversation Framework

### Setting Expectations (The "No Surprises" Rule)

**The Pre-Kickoff Email Template:**

```
Subject: [Customer Name] Decklar Kickoff — What to Expect

Hi [Name],

Looking forward to our kickoff call on [Date]. Here's what we'll accomplish:

✅ 15 minutes: Your shipment profile and requirements
✅ 15 minutes: Portal walkthrough and user setup  
✅ 15 minutes: First shipment planning
✅ 15 minutes: Q&A and next steps

What I'll need from you:
- List of lanes you want to track
- Key users who need portal access
- Any specific alert requirements

Goal by end of call: First test shipment scheduled.

Speak soon,
Jeff
```

### The Kickoff Call Structure (60 Minutes)

**Minutes 0-15: Relationship & Requirements**
- Confirm decision maker is on the call
- Review their stated goals from discovery
- Understand their shipment profile (volume, lanes, mode)
- Identify any changes since last conversation

**Minutes 15-30: Portal & Capabilities**
- Screen share: Live portal demo
- Focus on features relevant to their use case
- Set up user accounts live
- Show mobile experience

**Minutes 30-45: First Shipment Planning**
- Identify a specific shipment for first tracking
- Set expectations for what they'll see
- Configure alerts for that shipment
- Provide support contact info

**Minutes 45-60: Commitments & Next Steps**
- Confirm go-live date
- Lock in first check-in call (Day 3-5)
- Exchange contact info for urgent issues
- Send summary email within 2 hours

### The "Shadow Stakeholder" Question

Before ending kickoff:
> "Who else on your team needs to know this went well?"

This identifies:
- Internal champions you can leverage
- Potential blockers to address early
- Expansion opportunities (other departments)

## 5. The Check-In Conversation Framework

### The 3-Question Health Check

Every check-in should cover these three areas:

**1. Technical: "Is it working?"**
> "Any issues with tracking or alerts? Any shipments we missed?"

*Red flags:*
- Missed shipments = configuration issue
- Alert fatigue = threshold needs tuning
- Portal not used = adoption problem

**2. Value: "Are you seeing benefit?"**
> "Have you caught any incidents or prevented any issues yet?"

*If yes:* Document it. Get a quote. Ask for a case study.

*If no:*
> "Let's look at your data together. I want to make sure you're seeing everything."

**3. Expansion: "Who else needs this?"**
> "Who else on your team is asking about shipment visibility?"

*Listen for:*
- New departments
- New use cases
- New stakeholders (good or bad sign)

### The "Teach Me" Technique

Instead of explaining, ask them to explain:
> "Walk me through how you're using the dashboard day-to-day."

This reveals:
- Whether they're actually using it
- Workarounds they've created
- Features they're missing
- Training opportunities

## 6. The Expansion Conversation Framework

### The Expansion Signal Response Matrix

| Signal | Timing | Approach |
|--------|--------|----------|
| "This is great—my colleague in [dept] needs this" | Immediate | "Let's schedule a brief intro. What's their biggest visibility challenge?" |
| "Can you track [new use case]?" | Same call | "Absolutely—let's talk about your requirements and I'll get you a proposal by tomorrow." |
| "We're adding a new lane/route" | Within 24h | "Congratulations on the growth. Let's make sure Decklar scales with you." |
| "Do you integrate with [system]?" | Same week | "That's an enterprise feature—let me show you what's included in that tier." |
| Volume spike detected | Proactive outreach | "Saw your volume jumped—need any support scaling your tracking?" |

### The Expansion Conversation Structure

**Step 1: Anchor on Success**
> "You've been live for [time] and [specific result]. Let's talk about what's next."

**Step 2: Introduce the Gap**
> "Based on what you've shared, you're tracking [X] shipments, but I see an opportunity to also solve [Y]."

**Step 3: Paint the Picture**
> "If we added [capability], you could [specific benefit]. Another customer saw [result]."

**Step 4: Make it Concrete**
> "Here's what that would look like: [specific proposal with pricing]."

**Step 5: Create Urgency**
> "We could have this live by [specific date]. What would need to happen to make that real?"

### The "Land and Expand" Timeline

| Stage | Timing | Expansion Play |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| First Value | Day 30 | Add users, add lanes |
| Process Integration | Day 60 | Add capabilities (alerts, reporting) |
| Optimization | Day 90 | Volume discounts, contract extension |
| Strategic Partnership | Day 180+ | Multi-year agreements, enterprise tier |

## 7. The At-Risk Recovery Conversation

### The "Direct Acknowledgment" Approach

When a customer is at-risk, skip the subtlety:

**The Outreach:**
> "I noticed [specific metric changed]. I'm concerned we may not be delivering the value you expected. Can we talk about what's changed?"

**The Discovery Call:**
- What's changed in their business?
- What's changed in their priorities?
- What's changed in their experience with Decklar?

**The Recovery Commitments:**
- Specific fixes with dates
- Escalation path if issues recur
- Regular check-ins until resolved

**The Close:**
> "I'll send you a summary of everything we committed to. If we don't deliver, you have my direct line. But I'm confident we will."

### The "Exit Interview" (If They're Leaving)

If a customer is leaving, get intelligence:
> "I respect the decision. To help us improve—was it [price], [product], [support], or something else?"

Follow-up:
> "Would you be open to a call in 6 months to see if anything has changed?"

## 8. The QBR Presentation Framework

### The 6-Slide QBR Structure

**Slide 1: Executive Summary (1 minute)**
- Health score + trend
- One-sentence summary: "On track / Needs attention / At risk"
- Recommended action

**Slide 2: Usage Deep-Dive (2 minutes)**
- Shipments tracked vs. target
- Active users and engagement
- Feature utilization heatmap

**Slide 3: Value Delivered (2 minutes)**
- Incidents detected
- Claims prevented (documented)
- Customer quote

**Slide 4: What's Working / What's Not (2 minutes)**
- Operational excellence metrics
- Any service issues + resolution
- Open support tickets

**Slide 5: Expansion Opportunities (2 minutes)**
- New lanes detected
- Unused capabilities
- Volume growth projections

**Slide 6: Next 90 Days (1 minute)**
- Customer goals
- Decklar commitments
- Meeting schedule

### The QBR Question That Matters Most

End every QBR with:
> "On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to recommend Decklar to a colleague?"

Follow-up:
- 9-10: "What would make it a 10?" + ask for referral
- 7-8: "What would make it a 9?" + address gap
- <7: "What would make it a 9?" + recovery plan
