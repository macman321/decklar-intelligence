# Supply Chain Use Cases: A Complete Guide to IoT Visibility Applications

*Which supply chain visibility use case fits your operation? Learn how five distinct applications solve different business problems—and how to identify which one matches your needs.*

Not all supply chain visibility is the same. A pharmaceutical company tracking vaccines has fundamentally different requirements than an automotive manufacturer tracking returnable containers. Yet both use the same underlying technology: IoT-enabled tracking devices reporting location and environmental data in real time.

Understanding which use case (or use cases) apply to your operation is the first step to designing an effective visibility strategy. This guide breaks down the five primary Decklar supply chain applications—each with distinct pain points, success metrics, and ROI models.

---

## Use Case 1: High-Value Cargo Monitoring

**Best for:** Electronics manufacturers, pharmaceutical companies, luxury goods distributors

### The Problem

When a single shipment is worth $50,000–$500,000, visibility isn't optional—it's insurance against catastrophic loss. High-value cargo operations face three critical challenges:

- **Theft:** Organized cargo theft costs the industry $15–$30 billion annually. High-value electronics and pharmaceuticals are prime targets.
- **Damage:** Shock and temperature excursions can destroy product integrity without visible external damage.
- **Claims:** When something goes wrong, proving what happened (and when) determines whether insurance pays out.

Traditional tracking provides location—but location alone doesn't tell you if the cargo was handled roughly, exposed to extreme temperatures, or sitting in a facility longer than expected.

### The Decklar Solution

High-value cargo monitoring leverages the full sensor suite:

| Sensor | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **GPS** | Real-time location, route adherence, geofence alerts |
| **Temperature** | Detect excursions that could damage electronics or pharmaceuticals |
| **Shock/Accelerometer** | Identify rough handling, drops, or impacts |
| **Light** | Flag unauthorized container openings |
| **Humidity** | Detect moisture exposure that causes corrosion |

**Key capabilities:**
- Geofence alerts at origin, destination, and waypoints
- Immediate notification when temperature or shock thresholds are breached
- Complete data audit trail for insurance claims
- e-Proof of delivery with environmental conditions documented

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Visibility Rate | >95% | You can't protect what you can't see |
| Claims Reduction | 40–60% | Sensor data proves carrier liability |
| Recovery Rate | >60% | GPS enables rapid response to theft |
| Alert Response Time | <15 minutes | Fast action minimizes losses |

### ROI Calculation

A single prevented theft often pays for an entire year's monitoring program. Consider:
- Average shipment value: $100,000
- Monitoring cost per shipment: $15–$25
- Break-even: Prevent 1 theft per 4,000–6,000 shipments

Most high-value operations see **positive ROI within the first quarter**.

## Use Case 2: Cold Chain & Temperature-Sensitive Logistics

**Best for:** Food/beverage distributors, pharmaceutical companies, vaccine distributors, biologics manufacturers

### The Problem

Temperature excursions don't just damage products—they destroy them. A single cold chain break can render an entire shipment unusable:

- **Pharmaceuticals:** Many drugs require strict 2–8°C storage. Deviation for even 15 minutes can mean total loss.
- **Vaccines:** Ultra-cold chain requirements (-80°C for some COVID vaccines) demand specialized monitoring.
- **Fresh Produce:** Temperature spikes accelerate ripening and spoilage.

Beyond the direct product loss, regulatory compliance adds pressure. FDA regulations (21 CFR Part 11), EU GDP guidelines, and industry-specific standards require continuous temperature documentation.

### The Decklar Solution

Cold chain monitoring focuses on environmental sensors with configurable thresholds:

**Temperature Monitoring:**
- Continuous logging with 5-minute granularity
- Configurable thresholds per product type
- Immediate alerts on threshold breaches
- Historical data for compliance documentation

**Supporting Sensors:**
- **Humidity:** Critical for moisture-sensitive products (pharmaceuticals, electronics)
- **Light:** Indicates door openings that cause temperature spikes
- **Pressure:** (Select models) Tracks altitude changes for air cargo

### Industry-Specific Configurations

| Industry | Temperature Range | Critical Requirements |
|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Pharma (Standard) | 2–8°C | 21 CFR Part 11 compliance |
| Vaccines (Ultra-cold) | -80°C | Specialized sensors, battery optimization |
| Fresh Produce | 0–4°C | Ethylene detection for ripening |
| Chemicals | Varies by class | Freeze/thaw cycle tracking |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Excursions per 1,000 Shipments | <3 | Industry benchmark |
| Alert Response Time | <5 minutes | From threshold breach to notification |
| Compliance Audit Pass Rate | 100% | Documentation completeness |
| Spoilage Cost Reduction | 30–50% | Year-over-year comparison |

### The Compliance Advantage

Regulatory audits are inevitable. With proper IoT monitoring, you're not scrambling for documentation—you're handing over a complete digital record:
- Timestamped temperature logs
- Chain of custody verification
- Exception reports with root cause analysis
- Trending data showing process improvement

## Use Case 3: Multi-Modal International Shipments

**Best for:** Global manufacturers, automotive suppliers, aerospace components companies

### The Problem

International shipments pass through multiple handoffs—each creating a potential visibility gap:

- **Ocean freight:** 30–60 days with minimal visibility
- **Air freight:** Strict weight/size limits on tracking devices
- **Customs:** Delays create detention and demurrage fees
- **Carrier handoffs:** Responsibility transfers without data transfer

A shipment might travel truck → ocean → rail → truck across three countries with four different carriers. Traditional tracking gives you location at the origin and destination—with a massive black hole in between.

### The Decklar Solution

Multi-modal visibility requires specialized configuration:

**Device Characteristics:**
- Small, lightweight (air-freight compliant)
- 90-day battery life for ocean crossings
- International cellular connectivity
- Configurable ping rates for different transit modes

**Configuration Strategy:**
| Mode | Ping Frequency | Rationale |
|------|----------------|-----------|
| Truck (Origin) | 5–10 minutes | High visibility during critical loading phase |
| Ocean | 1–4 hours | Conserves battery during long transit |
| Air | 15–30 minutes | Compliance with flight regulations |
| Truck (Destination) | 5–10 minutes | Critical for unloading/delivery |

**Advanced Features:**
- Container tracking with BOL integration
- AWB (Air Waybill) field mapping
- Geofence alerts at customs checkpoints
- Dwell time tracking to flag delays

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Business Impact |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Visibility % Across Journey | 100% | No blind spots |
| Average Dwell Time at Ports | Baseline + 20% | Identify process improvements |
| Demurrage Fee Reduction | 50%+ | Catch delays early |
| Exception Resolution Time | <4 hours | Fast problem resolution |

### The Handoff Problem

The biggest challenge in multi-modal isn't technology—it's process. Decklar solves this by:
1. **Continuous tracking** regardless of carrier changes
2. **Single source of truth** for all shipment data
3. **Automated alerts** when shipments deviate from planned routes or timelines

## Use Case 4: Reverse Logistics & Returnable Asset Tracking

**Best for:** Automotive OEMs, appliance manufacturers, rental equipment companies, reusable packaging programs

### The Problem

Returnable assets—containers, pallets, totes—are supposed to come back. They often don't:

- **Attrition:** 30–40% annual loss is typical for unmanaged asset pools
- **Geographic Imbalance:** Assets accumulate at customer locations
- **Visibility Gap:** No tracking on the return journey
- **Billing Disputes:** Customers claim assets were returned; you have no proof

A major automotive manufacturer might have $10M+ in returnable containers in circulation. Losing track of even 5% annually is a $500,000 problem.

### The Decklar Solution

Reverse logistics requires a different device model:

**Reusable Bees (vs. Labels):**
- Designed for multiple trips (100+ cycles)
- Mountable on containers, pallets, or equipment
- Battery optimized for intermittent use over months/years

**Asset Flow Tracking:**
- Outbound: Track delivery to customer
- Inbound: Automatically detect return shipments
- Location: Know where every asset sits
- Utilization: Measure turn time and pool efficiency

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Calculation |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Asset Recovery Rate | >95% | (Returned / Shipped) × 100 |
| Average Turn Time | <30 days | Delivery to return cycle |
| Pool Utilization | >80% | Assets in motion vs. idle |
| Billing Dispute Resolution | >90% | GPS data settles disputes |

### The Billing Advantage

With asset tracking, billing disputes become data disputes—and data settles arguments:
- "We returned those containers last month."
- "Our records show they're still at your Dallas facility, received 47 days ago."

GPS location history trumps memory every time.

## Use Case 5: Last-Mile Delivery & Consumer-Facing Tracking

**Best for:** E-commerce retailers, 3PLs, final-mile carriers, white-glove delivery services

### The Problem

The last mile is the most expensive—and most visible—part of delivery:

- **Customer Service Load:** "Where's my order?" calls overwhelm support teams
- **Failed Deliveries:** Each failed attempt costs $15–$25
- **Proof of Delivery:** Disputes over whether delivery actually happened
- **Porch Piracy:** Packages stolen after delivery

Customers expect Amazon-level visibility for every shipment. Providing it traditionally required expensive integrations with carrier systems.

### The Decklar Solution

Last-mile visibility focuses on customer experience:

**Public Tracking Links:**
- Customers see real-time location without logging in
- Branded experience with your company logo
- Estimated delivery windows based on actual location

**e-Proof of Delivery:**
- Timestamp and GPS coordinates
- Optional photo of delivery location
- Digital signature capture
- Automatic email confirmation

**Exception Management:**
- Alerts for delivery attempts
- Redelivery scheduling
- Safe drop authorization

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Customer Service Calls | -30% | Self-service tracking reduces "where is it?" inquiries |
| First-Attempt Success Rate | >85% | Real-time ETAs help customers be available |
| Proof-of-Delivery Disputes | -50% | GPS + photo evidence |
| Customer Satisfaction | +15 points | Visibility drives satisfaction |

### The Experience Advantage

Last-mile tracking isn't just operational efficiency—it's marketing:
- **Trust:** Customers trust companies that provide visibility
- **Retention:** Delivery experience drives repeat purchase decisions
- **Reviews:** Positive delivery experiences generate reviews

## Choosing Your Use Case

Not sure which use case fits? Ask these questions:

**If considering High-Value Cargo:**
- What's the average value of a single shipment?
- How many theft or significant delay incidents per year?
- What's your current insurance claim process?

**If considering Cold Chain:**
- What temperature ranges are critical?
- Have you had temperature excursions? What was the cost?
- What regulatory requirements apply?

**If considering Multi-Modal:**
- What percentage of shipments involve multiple carriers?
- What's your average dwell time at ports/customs?
- How do you track international shipments today?

**If considering Reverse Logistics:**
- How many returnable assets do you have in circulation?
- What's your annual asset loss rate?
- How do you handle billing for unreturned assets?

**If considering Last-Mile:**
- What percentage of deliveries require multiple attempts?
- How many "where's my order" calls does CS handle daily?
- Do you offer customer-facing tracking today?

Every use case follows the same deployment pattern:

1. **Discovery:** Identify your specific pain points and success criteria
2. **Configuration:** Tune sensors and alerts for your use case
3. **Pilot:** Validate with 3–5 shipments before full rollout

The key is starting with one use case, proving value, and expanding from there.
