# Executive Briefing: Supply Chain Visibility in 15 Minutes

*A decision framework for C-suite and VP-level leaders evaluating IoT supply chain investments*

---

## The Bottom Line (60 Seconds)

**Modern supply chain visibility pays for itself.** Companies implementing IoT tracking typically see:

- **10-30% reduction** in logistics costs
- **40-60% decrease** in shipment exceptions requiring manual intervention  
- **90%+ improvement** in on-time delivery accuracy
- **Payback period** of 6-18 months

If your organization ships high-value goods, temperature-sensitive products, or operates across multiple carriers and modes, visibility isn't optional anymore—it's competitive infrastructure.

---

## What "Supply Chain Visibility" Actually Means

| Level | What You Get | Typical Cost | Best For |
|-------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **Carrier Tracking** | Status updates from your logistics providers | Included in freight | Low-value, non-critical shipments |
| **ERP Integration** | Centralized view of orders and inventory | $50K-$200K setup | Companies with complex order management |
| **IoT Real-Time Tracking** | Location, temperature, humidity, shock events at pallet/item level | $5-$25 per shipment | High-value, sensitive, or regulated cargo |
| **Predictive Analytics** | Exception forecasting, route optimization, demand sensing | Platform + data science | High-volume shippers with mature data practices |

**Most enterprises need layers 2-3.** Layer 4 is for companies already capturing rich IoT data.

## The Business Case: Three Ways Visibility Drives ROI

### 1. Cost Avoidance (Immediate)
- **Theft reduction:** Real-time alerts + geofencing cut cargo theft by 60-80%
- **Spoilage prevention:** Temperature excursions caught in minutes, not hours
- **Detention cost elimination:** Know exactly when shipments arrive/depart

### 2. Operational Efficiency (3-6 months)
- **Automated exception management:** 60% fewer "where's my shipment?" calls
- **Inventory optimization:** Reduce safety stock by 15-25% with confidence in transit times
- **Carrier scorecarding:** Data-driven negotiations and contract management

### 3. Revenue Protection (6-12 months)
- **Customer satisfaction:** Proactive communication beats reactive apologies
- **Contract compliance:** Prove SLAs met, avoid penalties, capture incentives
- **Premium service offerings:** Visibility as a customer-facing value-add

## Decision Framework: Do You Need IoT Visibility?

Score your organization on these criteria. If you hit **3 or more**, IoT visibility delivers measurable ROI:

| Criterion | Your Score |
|-----------|-----------|
| Ship products worth >$10K per pallet | ☐ |
| Temperature-sensitive cargo (food, pharma, chemicals) | ☐ |
| Customer penalties for late/incomplete delivery | ☐ |
| International shipments with multiple handoffs | ☐ |
| >$1M annual spend on expedited freight or replacements | ☐ |
| Regulatory compliance requirements (FDA, USDA, customs) | ☐ |
| Customers asking for real-time tracking data | ☐ |

**Scoring:**
- 0-2: Start with carrier tracking and ERP integration
- 3-4: Pilot IoT visibility on high-value lanes
- 5+: Full deployment justified on cost avoidance alone

## Implementation: What to Expect

### Week 1-2: Pilot Design
- Select 2-3 high-value shipping lanes
- Define success metrics (on-time %, exception detection, cost avoidance)
- Choose tracking technology (labels vs. reusable devices)

### Week 3-6: Pilot Execution
- Ship 20-50 tracked loads
- Train operations team on alert workflows
- Validate data accuracy against ground truth

### Week 7-10: Scale Decision
- Review pilot metrics
- Calculate ROI based on actual exception detection
- Plan phased rollout (typically 3-6 months for full deployment)

**Critical success factor:** Assign an internal champion with authority to change processes. Technology alone doesn't deliver ROI—operational change does.

## Vendor Evaluation Checklist

When comparing supply chain visibility vendors, assess:

### Technical Capabilities
- ☐ **Real-time data latency** (sub-15 minutes for location, sub-5 minutes for alerts)
- ☐ **Sensor coverage** (GPS, temperature, humidity, light, shock as needed)
- ☐ **Battery life** (disposable labels: 30-90 days; reusable: rechargeable)
- ☐ **Global connectivity** (cellular coverage in your shipping lanes)

### Integration & Operations
- ☐ **ERP connectivity** (pre-built connectors for your systems)
- ☐ **API access** (webhooks, REST, event streaming)
- ☐ **Alert configuration** (flexible thresholds, escalation rules)
- ☐ **Mobile access** (apps for drivers, warehouse staff, managers)

### Commercial Model
- ☐ **Hardware costs** (upfront vs. subscription)
- ☐ **Per-shipment pricing** (all-inclusive vs. à la carte)
- ☐ **Data retention** (how long history is stored)
- ☐ **Professional services** (implementation, training, ongoing support)

## Red Flags: Avoid These Pitfalls

### The "Tracking for Tracking's Sake" Trap
Visibility without operational workflows creates data noise. Before buying, map exactly who will respond to which alerts and how.

### The Integration Afterthought
Ask vendors: "Show me your pre-built connector for [your ERP]." Custom integrations add 3-6 months and $50K-$200K.

### The Hardware-Only Focus
Sensors are commodities. The platform—analytics, alerting, integration, support—is where value lives. Optimize for software maturity, not hardware specs.

### The Big Bang Rollout
Visibility projects fail when they try to track everything at once. Start with high-value, high-pain lanes. Prove ROI, then expand.

## Quick-Start Recommendations

### If You're Just Getting Started
1. **Audit your pain points:** Where do shipments go wrong today?
2. **Calculate cost of exceptions:** What's a late delivery, spoilage event, or theft incident actually costing you?
3. **Pilot on one lane:** Pick your highest-value, most problematic route
4. **Measure before and after:** Establish baseline metrics before adding visibility

### If You Have Carrier Tracking But Need More
1. **Identify blind spots:** Where does carrier data go dark? (handoffs, customs, warehouse dwell)
2. **Evaluate IoT solutions:** Focus on sensor types that match your cargo risks
3. **Plan for integration:** Visibility data is only valuable in your operational systems
4. **Build the business case:** Use pilot data to justify scale investment

### If You're Evaluating Vendors Now
1. **Request a pilot:** Any vendor confident in their platform will offer a trial
2. **Talk to references:** Ask specifically about integration timeline and support quality
3. **Test the alert system:** Simulate an exception. How fast does it reach the right person?
4. **Review the roadmap:** Supply chain tech evolves fast. Is the vendor investing?
