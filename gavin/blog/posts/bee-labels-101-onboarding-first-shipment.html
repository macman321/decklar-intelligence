Bee Labels 101: Onboarding Your First Shipment

Gavin • May 14, 2026 • 8 min read
← Back to Home

So, you've decided to join the visibility revolution—welcome! Whether you're a small business shipping your first international order or an enterprise logistics manager implementing a global tracking program, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about onboarding your first shipment with Bee Labels.

I'll be honest: the first time I set up a Bee Label shipment, I was equal parts excited and nervous. These tiny devices promise so much—real-time tracking, environmental monitoring, global connectivity—but would it actually work? Would I accidentally brick the device during activation? Would my shipment actually be trackable across oceans?

Spoiler alert: it worked beautifully. And yours will too. Let's dive in.

What Exactly Is a Bee Label?

Before we get into the how, let's talk about the what. A Bee Label is Decklar's flagship IoT tracking device—a credit-card-sized smart label that combines multiple sensors, cellular connectivity, and a ridiculously long-lasting battery into one elegant package.

Here's what each Bee Label can do:

  • GPS tracking with precision accuracy, updated as frequently as every 5 minutes (depending on your settings)
  • Temperature monitoring with alerts for excursions outside your defined thresholds
  • Humidity sensing for moisture-sensitive cargo
  • Light detection to alert you if a package is opened unexpectedly
  • Shock and tilt detection to catch rough handling or improper orientation
  • Global cellular connectivity that works in 150+ countries without needing local infrastructure
  • Up to 2 years of battery life under normal usage conditions

The magic is that all of this fits into something thinner than a smartphone and light enough to stick on a package without adding significant weight or bulk. Plus, Bee Labels are reusable—when your shipment arrives, simply collect the label and use it again.

Step 1: Unboxing and Initial Setup

Your Bee Labels arrive in a compact, environmentally-friendly package. Each label is individually sealed and includes a QR code and activation code. Before you do anything else, resist the urge to just stick it on a box and hope for the best. Trust me on this one.

First, download the Decklar mobile app or log into the web portal. Create an account if you haven't already (it takes about 2 minutes). Once you're in, look for the "Activate Device" option—you can scan the QR code on the label or manually enter the activation code printed on the back.

Here's where the magic starts. As soon as you activate the device, it begins establishing a connection to the cellular network. You'll see a status indicator in the app showing the activation progress. Usually, this takes 30-60 seconds. If it takes longer, don't panic—the device might just be finding the best signal.

Step 2: Configure Your Shipment

Once activated, it's time to tell the Bee Label what it's supposed to be monitoring. The Decklar platform makes this beautifully simple:

Set Your Route

Enter your origin and destination. This isn't just for mapping—it's how the system calculates estimated arrival times, identifies potential delays, and optimizes tracking frequency. You can enter addresses, airport codes, port codes, or even coordinates for remote locations.

Define Your Alerts

This is where you turn the Bee Label from a tracking device into a guardian angel. Set temperature thresholds (e.g., "Alert me if temperature goes below 2°C or above 8°C" for cold chain). Configure shock sensitivity based on your cargo type. Set geofences around critical waypoints. The more specific you are, the more useful your alerts will be.

Pro tip: Start conservative with your thresholds. You can always adjust them based on what you learn from your first few shipments. Nothing is worse than getting 47 "temperature excursion" alerts because you set your threshold at exactly the ambient temperature of your warehouse.

Customize Reporting

How often do you want updates? For high-value or time-sensitive shipments, every 5 minutes might make sense. For standard freight, every hour is probably fine. You can also set different reporting frequencies for different modes of transport—more frequent during air transit, less frequent during ocean shipping, for example.

Step 3: Physical Placement

Now for the hands-on part. Where you place the Bee Label matters more than you might think.

Do:

  • Place it on an exterior surface where it has clear line-of-sight to GPS satellites
  • Keep it away from metal containers that could interfere with cellular signals
  • Mount it securely using the industrial-strength adhesive—this thing needs to survive rough handling
  • For temperature monitoring, place it where it will reflect the actual cargo temperature, not just ambient conditions

Don't:

  • Bury it inside a pallet or container where it can't get a signal
  • Attach it to surfaces that will exceed 60°C (the Bee Label will survive, but your data might get wonky)
  • Forget to check that the adhesive is making full contact—loose labels are lost labels

Step 4: Test and Verify

Before your shipment leaves, do a quick sanity check. Open the Decklar app and verify you're getting location updates and sensor readings. If you have time, simulate an alert condition—walk outside with the package and see if the location updates, or briefly expose it to a different temperature and confirm the sensor responds.

This 2-minute test can save you hours of anxiety later. There's nothing quite like the sinking feeling of realizing your tracking device wasn't actually tracking.

Step 5: Monitor and Respond

Once your shipment is in transit, the real value of the Bee Label kicks in. You'll get real-time updates through the Decklar dashboard, mobile app, or via API integration with your existing systems.

Pay attention to those alerts you configured. When you get a temperature excursion notification, don't just acknowledge it—investigate. Contact your carrier, check if the cargo needs immediate attention, document everything. The data from your Bee Label isn't just for peace of mind; it's evidence for insurance claims, ammunition for vendor negotiations, and intelligence for process improvements.

The Payoff

When your shipment arrives safely at its destination, and you have a complete digital record of its journey—every temperature reading, every location ping, every shock event—you'll understand why I get so excited about this technology. This isn't just tracking; it's transformation.

Retrieve your Bee Label (it's designed to be easily removable), reset it for the next shipment, and pat yourself on the back. You've just joined the elite club of supply chain professionals who actually know what's happening with their cargo.
